Honest start

Where this went wrong.

I underestimated how complex this project would be.

This is the first time I've led a project this technical. I didn't see the requirements, the dependencies, or the roadblocks ahead of time. The original timeline I set came from that gap, not from negligence.

AI helped me move fast, but it couldn't tell me what I didn't know to ask.

We missed the original launch window. I owe Adam and the leadership team an apology for the delay.

What changed

What I learned.

Naming the gaps directly so the same trap doesn't catch the next project.

Lesson 01

Technical projects need technical foresight from day one.

A senior dev should be in the planning room before the timeline is set, not after. They see the dependencies a non-technical PM can't see yet.

Lesson 02

AI moves you fast on the path you can already see.

It doesn't show you the path you're missing. The blind spots are still on the person who's planning, not the tool.

Lesson 03

"Done by Friday" is not a plan.

Real plans have phases, hand-offs, and named owners per step. I'll bring that level of structure to every technical project from now on.

Lesson 04

Buffer days are not optional.

If every day is allocated, the schedule has no shock absorber. The plan we have today builds buffer into every week.

Where Flizow stands today

What's built.

Six surfaces leadership will care about. The rest of the app supports these.

Client home

Every client in one place. Status at a glance. Six tabs per client (overview, onboarding, about, stats, notes, touchpoints) so account managers stop digging through Drive folders to find what they need.

Per-service kanban

A working kanban board per client service. Drag-and-drop, work-in-progress limits, and capacity-aware booking so we stop assigning Sarah eight cards on a day she's already at capacity.

Weekly WIP

The whole weekly meeting flow in one surface. Auto-built agenda, live-meeting timer, captured notes / decisions / actions. The meeting ends and the follow-ups are already in the system.

Capacity heatmap

Workspace-wide view of who's slammed this week and who has room. Green / amber / red zones across ten weekdays. Booking against soft and hard caps so overbooking gets flagged before it ships.

Service templates

Reusable blueprints for every kind of work the agency does. Spinning up a new service inherits onboarding, brief, and phase cards in one click. Setup that used to take an hour takes a minute.

Safety net

Every delete is recoverable. Five-second undo on any action, then 90 days in the trash bin. Nobody has to live in fear of misclicking. Nothing is gone for good unless we mean it.

Eyes open

Risks, and what I need.

The places this can still go sideways, and the three asks I need from leadership for the plan above to hold.

Top three risks

  • Senior dev wraps later than planned. Adding Account settings to his scope pushes hand-off to Tue / Wed. If anything slips here, every downstream date moves with it.
  • No rollback strategy. If launch goes sideways the senior dev hotfixes live. The Friday May 15 soft launch is the de-risker: 72 hours of real use before the leadership briefing.
  • Hard launch date. May 18 leadership presentation is on the calendar. Everything upstream has to hit its mark. There's no "we'll move it a week."

What I need from leadership

  • Approve the May 18 launch with phased rollout. Leadership Mon AM. One team per day Mon-Thu. No big-bang day where we present and roll out everyone simultaneously.
  • Allow the Friday May 15 soft launch. One internal team gets early access for 72 hours. Risk insurance for the leadership presentation.
  • Acknowledge May 19–22 as hotfix-readiness mode. Senior dev on call for fixes, no other commitments. Bugs found in the first week of real use get patched fast.
